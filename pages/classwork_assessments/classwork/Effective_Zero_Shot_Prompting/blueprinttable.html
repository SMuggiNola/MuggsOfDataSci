🧱 S2 Blueprint Table

Structural Element Purpose Why It’s Ideal for Mistral 7B
Model Identity
“You are a professor of…”		 Establishes expertise, tone, and domain vocabulary. Mistral 7B relies heavily on explicit role priming —it doesn’t infer professional tone automatically like larger models.
User Role
“I am a high-school student…”		 Clarifies audience and purpose so responses stay focused and readable. Smaller models over-explain or wander without a clear audience; this fixes that.
Goal Statement
“My goal: determine whether…”		 Defines the task in measurable terms. Keeps reasoning bounded; Mistral 7B performs best with one explicit objective.
Rules (1–3 lines) Encodes boundaries (“Do NOT summarize…”). Negative instructions are especially effective with mid-size models —they prevent drift.
Explicit Output Format
“Relevance: … Reason: …”		 Forces a predictable structure for parsing or logging. Mistral 7B benefits from clear formatting cues that prevent narrative tangents.
Objective + Input Section Separates instructions from data. Layout cues (“Objective: … Article Chunk: …”) help the model know when to stop reading directions and start reasoning.
